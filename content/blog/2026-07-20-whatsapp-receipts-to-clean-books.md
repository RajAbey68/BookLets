If you manage a short-term rental, you already know where your receipts actually live: not in a shoebox, not in an app you remembered to open at the till, but scattered across a WhatsApp thread with your cleaner, your handyman, or a supplier. A photo of a hardware store receipt. A screenshot of a linen invoice. A voice note about the plumber's callout fee.

Every one of those is a real expense. Every one of them needs to end up on your books, categorized correctly, before tax time turns it into a scramble.

## The old way: retype everything

The usual fix is manual: open WhatsApp, find the photo, save it, open your bookkeeping tool, upload it, type in the amount, pick a category, repeat. For a host with a handful of properties, that's an hour lost every week to data entry that a computer should be doing.

## The BookLets way: export the chat, upload the zip

BookLets now reads WhatsApp chat exports directly. Export any conversation as a `.zip` (WhatsApp's built-in "Export Chat" feature, with media included), drop it into BookLets, and the system:

1. **Unpacks every image** in the export and finds the ones that look like receipts.
2. **Extracts the vendor, amount, and date** from each receipt automatically.
3. **Stages them as draft journal entries** for you to review — nothing posts to your ledger without a human looking at it first.
4. **Shows live, number-by-number progress** while it works, so a 200-photo export doesn't feel like it hung.

You review the drafts, approve the ones that look right, and correct anything the extraction got wrong. What used to be an hour of retyping becomes a few minutes of skimming.

## Why the draft step matters

It would be faster to post everything straight to the ledger automatically. We don't do that, on purpose. Financial records are the one place where "probably right" isn't good enough — a misread amount or a duplicate receipt compounds into a wrong P&L. Every AI-assisted entry lands in a review queue first, in plain sight, before it touches your real books.
