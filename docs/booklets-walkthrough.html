Section 1

What BookLets is for

A single place to record income and expenses for the villa, attribute revenue to the correct month, produce a clean general ledger, and hand monthly numbers to the accountant for QuickBooks Online or statutory filing.

Replaces

Loose spreadsheets for petty-cash analysis; manual re-keying of monthly totals; ad-hoc reconciliation between operator and accountant.

Feeds

QuickBooks Online via CSV export. The system is the source of truth for the books; QBO is the canonical filing surface.

Books in

LKR (Sri Lankan rupees). Every transaction is recorded in LKR exactly as it happened.

Reports in

USD using the spot FX rate on the day the month is closed (preferred: monthly average if a daily feed is available).

One golden rule — When a guest's stay crosses a month boundary, the entire stay revenue is booked to the check-out month. No day-by-day apportionment. This is because refund risk effectively closes on check-out.
Section 2

The monthly workflow at a glance

#StepWhere in BookLetsOwner
1 Record petty-cash and operating expenses on the operator's monthly workbook (Income & Petty Cash Analysis). External spreadsheet (today) Villa captain
2 At month end, upload the workbook. BookLets parses every row, classifies it to a chart-of-accounts code, and shows a preview. /imports Bookkeeper
3 Review the preview: per-section totals, any unmapped columns, rows flagged with warnings. Fix the source workbook and re-upload if anything is off. /imports preview Bookkeeper
4 Confirm & post. BookLets writes balanced double-entry journal entries to the general ledger. Idempotent: re-uploading the same file does not duplicate. /imports (P2 — next phase) Bookkeeper
5 Reconcile to bank. Match cleared transactions; queue exceptions. /reconcile (P4 — planned) Bookkeeper
6 Run the FX revaluation at month close (LKR → USD). Month-close action (P5) Accountant
7 Export the ledger CSV and import into QuickBooks Online. Export CSV button Accountant
Screen 1

Sign-in Live

Access is by Google account only, and only emails on the allow-list can sign in.

What you'll see

  • A centred card with the BookLets eyebrow, a "Sign in" heading, and a single "Continue with Google" button.
  • Clear error messages if your email is not on the allow-list, or if the system is misconfigured.

What you need to do

  1. Send your Google address to the operator.
  2. They add it to the allow-list.
  3. Visit the site, click Continue with Google, and you'll land on the Dashboard.
Security note — In production, if the allow-list is accidentally cleared, sign-in fails for everyone (fail-closed). This is deliberate: the operator would rather lock themselves out than let an unauthorised account into the books.
Screen 2

Dashboard Live

First screen after sign-in. A one-glance view of how the portfolio is performing this month and year-to-date.

What's on it

Total Revenue

Month-to-date gross income recognised in the ledger.

Net Income

Revenue minus operating expenses. Margin shown in the sub-label.

ADR / RevPAR

Average daily rate and revenue per available room — STR yield metrics.

Portfolio Occupancy

% of available room-nights actually sold this month.

Below the cards

  • Receipt uploader — drop a photo or scan of a receipt; gets attached to an expense (handwritten / printed flagged separately — see Roadmap, P8).
  • Revenue Trend bar chart — gross revenue and net income side by side for recent months.
  • Property Yield list — per-villa headline numbers with a link through to the Properties page.

Buttons in the header

  • Download Report — exports the ledger CSV for the period.
  • + Create Entry — jumps to the General Ledger to add a manual journal entry.

Screen 4

Bookings Live

A single ordered table of every reservation, regardless of which channel it came through.

Columns

ColumnWhat it tells you
IDHostaway booking reference, or BookLets' internal short ID if the booking was created manually.
PropertyWhich villa.
ChannelBooking.com, Airbnb, direct, etc.
Check In / Check OutStay dates. The check-out date determines which month the revenue is booked to.
TotalGross reservation value, in the channel's billing currency.
StatusColoured badge: Confirmed, Completed, Pending, Cancelled.

Actions

  • + Create Booking — manual entry for direct bookings that didn't come through a channel.
  • Hostaway sync (from the Sidebar) pulls in new and updated channel bookings.
Revenue recognition reminder — Status moves to Completed on check-out. Revenue is recognised at that point and falls into the check-out month, even if the stay started in the previous month.
Screen 5

General Ledger Live

The double-entry journal. Every income and expense in BookLets shows up here, in chronological order, with full debit/credit detail.

Columns

ColumnWhat it tells you
DateTransaction date (not posting date — when it actually happened).
ReferenceAn 8-character ID that links every line of a single journal entry together. All lines with the same Reference balance to zero.
AccountThe chart-of-accounts line item, e.g. 4000 Rent Income or 6200 Electricity.
MemoDescription of the transaction as entered by the bookkeeper or imported from the spreadsheet.
DebitGreen column. Increases assets and expenses; decreases income and liabilities.
CreditRed column. Increases income and liabilities; decreases assets and expenses.

Filters & export

  • Period filter — dropdown auto-populated with every month that has activity, plus "All Time". Defaults to All Time.
  • Export CSV — single click; produces a file ready to import into QuickBooks Online. Columns map cleanly to QBO's journal-entry import format.
Screen 6

Imports — Spreadsheet Uploader Preview

Where you upload the monthly Income & Petty Cash Analysis workbook. Currently a read-only preview; posting to the ledger comes in the next phase.

How it works

  1. Drop the .xlsx for the month into the uploader. Limit 10 MB.
  2. BookLets parses every row, reads the column headers, and matches each amount to a chart-of-accounts code.
  3. A preview appears below the form. Nothing is written to the ledger until the confirm-and-post step (P2, next phase).

What the preview shows you

Summary cardPeriod label, total rows parsed, net amount (income − expense), and a file fingerprint so you can confirm re-uploads.
Totals by account, per sectionOne mini-table per spreadsheet section: Prior-Month Catch-up, Daily, Recurring, Accruals, Accrual Reversals, Prepayments, Prepayment Reversals.
Unmapped columnsAmber banner listing any column header BookLets couldn't match to a chart account. Those amounts are ignored until the mapping is added.
Per-section row tablesEvery row, with date, description, petty-cash top-up, postings list (account code + column + amount), and any quality warnings.

Warning flags you may see

  • Date forward-filled — date was blank, filled from the previous row (shown with an orange asterisk).
  • Petty-cash > LKR 5,000 without memo — petty-cash convention requires a memo above this threshold.
  • Amount in unmapped column — header needs to be added to the chart mapping.
  • Evidence-quality flag — description contains "no receipt", "handwritten", etc. Useful for receipt chase-up.

Key accounting policies

TopicPolicy
CurrencyBooks in LKR. USD reporting via FX rate on month-close date (preferred: monthly average if a daily feed is available).
Revenue recognitionEntire stay revenue booked to the check-out month. No day-by-day apportionment.
Petty cashHeld by the villa captain. Top-ups recorded against 1010. Items above LKR 5,000 require a memo.
PayrollGross salary split into 6100 Salaries, plus statutory deductions 2200 APIT (income tax), 2210 EPF (8% employee + 12% employer), 2220 ETF (3% employer). Net wages to 6110.
FrameworkSLFRS (Sri Lanka Financial Reporting Standards) is the canonical default. Swappable to other GAAP/IFRS standards if the portfolio expands overseas.
